# PRODUCT.md

## Product Purpose

Omakase is a portable quality standard for AI agent work: one skill, twelve non-negotiable rules, a critique rubric, and persistent project memory (`.omakaseagent/taste.md`, `decisions.md`). It installs into any agent harness (Claude Code, Cursor, OpenCode, Codex, Grok) and enforces senior craftsmanship on everything an agent ships. The name is literal: omakase means "I'll leave it up to you" — you state the goal, the chef decides how, and nothing mediocre reaches the table.

## Users

Senior software engineers and technical leads who use AI coding agents daily and are tired of generic, hedging, over-engineered output ("slop"). They value taste, brevity, and proof over volume. They read source before READMEs and judge tools in the first ten seconds.

## Brand & Tone

- Voice: confident, terse, opinionated. A head chef, not a waiter. "Trust the chef." "Zero slop." Short declaratives over marketing fluff.
- The sushi/kaiseki metaphor is native to the product, not decoration: courses, the counter, the chef's standard, the menu.
- Aesthetic lane: retro shōwa-era Japanese poster / matchbox-label illustration (cream paper, vermillion, ink black, pink; checkered borders, panel compositions, halftone speckle) fused with the structure of a fine-dining kaiseki menu, with brushed sumi-ink accents.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient heroes, icon-card grids, "Supercharge your workflow" copy.
- Dark "AI tool" sites: neon glow on black, glassmorphism, particle backgrounds.
- Cute anime/kawaii Japan pastiche. The reference is mid-century print design, not mascots.

## Strategic Principles

- Quality over speed; the excellence gate is the product.
- Specialization beats generalization; you talk to leads, leads delegate.
- Every claim on a marketing surface must be true of the repo (real commands, real file names, real rules).
