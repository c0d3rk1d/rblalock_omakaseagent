# Omakase Taste Memory

## What Good Looks Like Here
- Ruthless simplicity and "code judo" — deleting complexity is celebrated
- Direct, confident communication with zero AI fluff or politeness theater
- Every non-trivial output includes clear "Why this approach" reasoning
- Artifacts that feel like they were made by a senior expert who has strong, earned taste
- The system itself must pass the Critique Rubric (we eat our own dogfood)

## What We Reject
- Generic AI slop (unnecessary comments, defensive code, `any` casts, over-abstraction)
- Files that grow past healthy size boundaries without strong justification
- Vague or hedged language when standards are being enforced
- Clever solutions when a simpler, more direct one exists
- Anything that would fail the 8-bullet Omakase Critique Rubric

## Current Standards
- Critique gate is mandatory on all significant work
- Persistent taste and decisions are first-class context
- The Engineering persona is the default for code-related tasks
- We ship what we would actually use daily at the highest standard
